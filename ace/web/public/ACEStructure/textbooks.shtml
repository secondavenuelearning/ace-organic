Textbooks

An instructor may choose to write a textbook embedded into ACE. The textbook consists of chapters, each of which contains any number of pieces of content. A piece of content may be text, a Marvin picture, an image, a movie, Jmol imput, or an ACE question for a student to practice. ACE allows the author to add, remove, reorder, or edit the chapters and content.

The author of an embedded textbook may choose to add coauthors to it. The author and the coauthors can all edit the textbook, but only one can do so at any one time. If a coauthor of a textbook browses the question database to add a practice question to the textbook, he or she will see questions that were locally authored or modified by the original author of the textbook, not by himself or herself. Students reading the textbook will also see the questions as locally authored or modified by the original textbook author, even if the original textbook author is not the instructor of their course.

The author of an embedded textbook may also choose to make that textbook visible to other instructors. If a second instructor then decides to edit that textbook, ACE makes a copy of it and assigns it to the second instructor. Subsequently, any changes that the original instructor makes to the original textbook will be invisible to the second instructor, and vice versa. If the original author has incorporated any locally authored questions (i.e., question that are not part of the master database) into the textbook, those questions will be inaccessible to the new author, and he or she must replace or remove them.

An instructor may choose to associate a course with an embedded textbook. The textbook may be either one that he or she has written, or one that another author has written and chosen to make visible to all instructors. The instructor chooses the book from the pulldown menu on the course-editing page. When an instructor has associated a textbook with a course, a Textbook tab appears when a user enters that course. ACE does not record students' work on questions that have been incorporated into the textbook.
